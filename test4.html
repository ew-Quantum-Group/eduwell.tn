Articles: A, An, The, √ò

Definite and indefinite article usage

A2

Articles are determiners indicating specific (the) or non-specific (a/an) reference. Use a/an with singular countable nouns on first mention; the when both parties know the referent.

‚ú¶ I saw a dog in the park. (first mention ‚Äî any dog)
‚ú¶ The dog was very friendly. (known reference)
‚ú¶ an before vowel sounds: an apple, an hour, an MBA

Use no article (√ò) with: plural/uncountable nouns in general statements, languages, academic subjects, meals, sports, proper nouns.
√ò Dogs are loyal. She studies √ò physics at √ò university.
